Mango

Suitable factors required for the cultivation of Mango are : 1. Temperature, 2. Rainfall, 3. Soils, 4. Fertilizers and 5.Labour !

Temperature:

Mango is a temperate crop but can be grown under a wide range of climatic conditions.The optimum mean shade temperatures of about 28 Â°C.Temperature plays a direct role on the flowering, fruit set and fruit development in mango.

Rainfall:

It can withstand both dry conditions and heavy rainfall. The mangos is a native of monsoon lands and prefers a climate with 75-250 cm rainfall concentrated from June to September

Soils:

Mango can be grown in the presence of small amounts of kankar in neutral or slightly alkaline soils upto pH of 7.5 may not harm the tree.The soils should preferably have a very low total water soluble salt content (0.04 to 0.05%).

Fertilizers:

A young tree that is planted in the garden should receive 3 applications of 10-20-20 (N-P-K) fertilizer, totaling about 0.5 kg, spread throughout the year. Mature trees should receive fertilizer just before blooming and after fruiting. Use a 6-6-6-2 or 8-3-9-2 (N-P-K-Mg) fertilizer.
