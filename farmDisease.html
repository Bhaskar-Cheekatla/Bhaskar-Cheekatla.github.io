RICE CROP DISEASES

1. Sheath Blight:

- Symptoms: Observe long, dark lesions on the sheaths of rice plants.

- Causes&effect: Can lead to rotting and reduced yield.

- Better Growth: Maintain proper water levels, avoid excessive nitrogen, and space plants.

- Pesticides: Use fungicides like azoxystrobin or tebuconazole.





2. Blast Disease:

- Symptoms: Look for elliptical lesions on leaves and panicles.

- Causes&effect: Can cause wilting and yield loss.

- Better Growth: Practice field sanitation and balanced fertilization.

- Pesticides: Consider fungicides like tricyclazole or carbendazim.





3. Brown Spot:

- Symptoms: Small, round, brown spots on leaves.

- Causes&effect: Can reduce photosynthesis and yield.

- Better Growth: Maintain proper spacing between plants and water management.

- Pesticides: Fungicides like propiconazole or epoxiconazole can help.

WHEAT CROP DISEASES

1. Rust Diseases (Stripe Rust, Leaf Rust, Stem Rust):

- Symptoms: Recognized by reddish-brown pustules on leaves, stems, and wheat heads.

- Causes&effect: Weaken the plant and reduce yield.

- Better Growth: Plant in well-drained soil, provide proper spacing, and balanced fertilization.

- Pesticides: Fungicides like triazoles and strobilurins are effective.





2. Powdery Mildew:

- Symptoms: Characterized by white, powdery spots on leaves.

- Causes&effect: Impair plant growth and grain development.

- Better Growth: Avoid overcrowding, practice good soil management.

- Pesticides: Fungicides can be applied when necessary.





3. Fusarium Head Blight (Scab):

- Symptoms: Visible as pink or white mold on wheat heads.

- Causes&effect: Reduces grain quality and yield.

- Better Growth: Select disease-resistant wheat varieties.

- Pesticides: Consult with experts for appropriate fungicide use.





5. Tan Spot:

- Symptoms: Visible as pink or white mold on wheat heads.

- Causes&effect: Reduces grain quality and yield.

- Better Growth: Select disease-resistant wheat varieties.

- Pesticides: Consult with experts for appropriate fungicide use.

VEGETABLE CROP DISEASES

1. Powdery Mildew:

- Symptoms: Look for white, powdery spots on leaves and stems.

- Causes&effect: Reduced growth and yield, leaves may wither.

- Better Growth: Proper spacing, good air circulation, and disease-resistant varieties.

- Pesticides: Consider sulphur or neem oil-based products.





2. Early Blight::

- Symptoms: Observe dark spots with concentric rings on leaves.

- Causes&effect: Leaf yellowing, reduced fruit production

- Better Growth: Mulching, avoiding overhead watering, and crop rotation.

- Pesticides: Copper-based fungicides can be effective.





3. Aphids:

- Symptoms: Small, soft-bodied insects, often in clusters on plants.

- Causes&effect: Stunted growth, distorted leaves, honeydew secretion.

- Better Growth: Introduce beneficial insects like ladybugs, and use reflective mulch.

- Pesticides: Insecticidal soaps or neem oil can help control aphids.





5. Tomato Late Blight:

- Symptoms: Observe dark, water-soaked lesions on leaves and fruit.

- Causes&effect: Rapid plant decay, fruit rotting.

- Better Growth: Proper plant spacing, good air circulation, and disease-resistant tomato varieties.

- Pesticides: Copper-based fungicides or mancozeb can be used.

FRUIT CROP DISEASES

1. Panama Disease:

- Symptoms: Wilting of leaves, Yellowing of leaves and Stunted growth

- Causes&effect: Caused by soil borne fungus called Fusarium. It leads to reduced nutrient uptake and eventually death

- Better Growth: Use disease-resistant banana varieties, Ensure proper soil drainage and avoid waterlogging

- Pesticides: Pesticides are not effective against panama disease. It's best to focus on prevention and avoid planting bananas in the same area repeatedly.





2. Citrus Canker:

- Symptoms: Small, raised lesions on citrus leaves and fruit.

- Causes&effect: Reduced fruit quality, leaf drop, and defoliation.

- Better Growth: Maintain proper spacing, regular pruning, and balanced fertilization.

- Pesticides: Copper-based sprays or bactericides may help.





3. Grape Downy Mildew:

- Symptoms: Look for oil-like spots on grape leaves.

- Causes&effect: Reduced grape quality and yield.

- Better Growth: Prune vines for sunlight penetration, control humidity, and practice canopy management.

- Pesticides: Consider fungicides like mancozeb or copper-based products.
