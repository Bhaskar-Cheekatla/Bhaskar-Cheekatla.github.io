Chillies

Suitable factors required for the cultivation of Chillies are : 1. Temperature, 2. Rainfall, 3. Soils, 4. Fertilizers and 5.Technology !

Temperature:

Chilli is a tropical and subtropical type of plant, and it requires a combination of a warm and humid environment to grow. The ideal temperature is considered 20-25°C. Temperatures above 37⁰C adversely effects chilli plant growth. However, for fruit maturity, dry weather is ideal.

Rainfall:

Chilli crop is raised as rainfed crop where the amount of annual rainfall is around 80-100 cm and well distributed.A well-drained, well-aerated, fairly light fertile loam with a fair moisture holding capacity is ideal.

Soils:

An ideal soil for chilli is light loamy or sandy loam rich in lime and organic matters. Strongly acid and alkaline soils are not suitable for chilli growing.

Fertilizers:

Nitrogen requirement for chillies is 120 kg/ha. Apply 30 Kg basally before planting. Apply the balance , on 30,60 and 90 days after transplanting at equal doses of 30 Kg each and irrigate.

Technology:

The various machineries have been adopted by farmers for chilli production. The tractor, power tiller, mini tiller, trailers and cleaner equipments consume 49.50%, 105, 5.5%, 30% and 4%, respectively.
