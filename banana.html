Banana

Suitable factors required for the cultivation of Banana are : 1. Temperature, 2. Rainfall, 3. Soils, 4. Fertilizers and 5.Labour !

Temperature:

Bananas are mainly grown in the tropical parts where temperatures do not fall below 16 °C.he optimum temperature is 31 or 32°C, a temperature reached only in the summer months in the banana growing districts. Leaf emergence stops at about 9 or 10°C.

Rainfall:

Banana requires rainfall below 150 cm. The coastal plains and the irrigated tracts in the peninsular parts provide ideal climate for banana cultivation.

Soils:

The soil suitable for banana should be 0.5 1m in depth, rich, well drained, fertile, moisture retentive, containing plenty of organic matter. The range of pH should be 6.5-7.5. Alluvial and volcanic soils are the best for banana cultivation.

Fertilizers:

Banana crop requires 7-8 Kg N, 0.7- 1.5 Kg P and 17-20 Kg K per metric tonne yield. Banana responds well to application of nutrients. Traditionally farmers use more of urea and less of phosphorous and potash.

Labour:

Growing bananas is, in general, labour intensive. It involves clearing jungle growth, propping of the plants to counter bending from the weight of the growing fruit, and irrigation in some regions.
